Janasena Party

The Janasena Party (JSP) is a regional political party in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh. It was founded on 14 March 2014 by Telugu film actor and producer Pawan Kalyan. The party's ideology is based on the principles of social justice, equality, and development.

Main Goals

  • Achieve social justice and equality for all citizens, regardless of caste, religion, gender, or region.
  • Promote economic development and create jobs for the unemployed.
  • Improve the quality of life for all citizens by providing access to basic amenities such as education, healthcare, and housing.

Achievements

In the 2019 Andhra Pradesh Legislative Assembly election, the JSP contested 140 seats and won 21 seats. The party also polled 6.4% of the popular vote.

Telugu Desam Party (TDP)

The Telugu Desam Party (TDP) is a regional political party in India. It was founded on 29 March 1982 by N. T. Rama Rao, a Telugu film actor and producer. The TDP's ideology is based on the principles of social justice, regionalism, and development.

Main Goals

  • Achieve social justice and equality for all citizens, regardless of caste, religion, gender, or region.
  • Promote economic development and create jobs for the unemployed.
  • Improve the quality of life for all citizens by providing access to basic amenities such as education, healthcare, and housing.

Achievements

In the 2019 Andhra Pradesh Legislative Assembly election, the TDP won 23 seats.

YSR Congress Party (YSRCP)

The YSR Congress Party (YSRCP) is a regional political party in India. It was founded on 9 March 2009 by Y. S. Jagan Mohan Reddy, the son of former Andhra Pradesh Chief Minister Y. S. Rajasekhara Reddy. The YSRCP's ideology is based on the principles of social justice, regionalism, and development.

Main Goals

  • Achieve social justice and equality for all citizens, regardless of caste, religion, gender, or region.
  • Promote economic development and create jobs for the unemployed.
  • Improve the quality of life for all citizens by providing access to basic amenities such as education, healthcare, and housing.

Achievements

In the 2019 Andhra Pradesh Legislative Assembly election, the YSRCP won 151 seats and formed the government. The party also polled 50.1% of the popular vote.
